Arctic PASSION Deliverables Matrix

A comprehensive overview of project deliverables by work package.

Work Package 1

D1.1: Report on Missing Elements for an Improved Arctic Observing System

Legacy Value: Identifies structural and thematic gaps that hinder a fully integrated pan-AOSS, providing the foundation for future investments and cross-sector coordination.

Legacy Recommendations: Immediate: Disseminate findings to Arctic coordination entities (e.g., SAON, AMAP, EPCO). Mid-Term: Organize stakeholder workshops to validate findings and discuss implementation. Long-Term: Institutionalize findings in future roadmap cycles and align funding calls accordingly.

Concrete Next Step: Host a thematic session during the next Arctic Observing Summit to prioritize actionable responses.

Responsible Organizations: SAON and the European Polar Coordination Office (EPCO), with contributions from Arctic Council working groups and EU Polar Cluster partners.

D1.2: Definition for the Pilot Set of Essential Arctic Variables (EAV/SAVs)

Legacy Value: Sets foundational guidelines for Arctic observations by defining EAV/SAVs to ensure consistency in data collection for a unified, sustainable system.

Legacy Recommendations: Involve key stakeholders (AMAP, WMO, SIOS, Indigenous organizations, national institutions). Immediate: Engage key institutions. Mid-Term: Regularly update definitions via expert workshops. Long-Term: Institutionalize these guidelines globally.

Concrete Next Step: Organize an international expert workshop to validate and adopt the proposed variables.

Responsible Organizations: SAON, in close collaboration with WMO and SIOS.

D1.3: Website with Protocols, Planning, and Reporting Tools

Legacy Value: Provides a centralized digital platform to facilitate long-term collaboration, reporting, and coordination among researchers and stakeholders.

Legacy Recommendations: Immediate: Ensure hosting, security, and regular updates. Mid-Term: Formalize collaborative partnerships. Long-Term: Institutionalize the platform within existing Arctic data networks.

Concrete Next Step: Secure sustainable funding and technical resources to maintain the platform post-project.

Stakeholders: NPI, international Arctic research institutions, national and European funding agencies, Arctic research community, Indigenous and local communities, EU Polar Cluster.

Responsible Organizations: Norwegian Polar Institute (NPI) in partnership with SAON, EU Polar Cluster, or an Arctic Council Working Group.

D1.6: FRM Surface Temperature Datasets and Recommendations

Legacy Value: Provides quality-controlled FRM datasets crucial for accurate climate monitoring and data integration across Arctic observation networks.

Legacy Recommendations: Immediate: Ensure dataset availability and dissemination. Mid-Term: Promote adoption of FRM methodologies. Long-Term: Institutionalize the production and use of FRM datasets.

Concrete Next Step: Integrate these datasets into global data services (e.g., Copernicus Climate Change Service).

Stakeholders: WMO, Copernicus services, ESA, European Environmental Agency, national meteorological institutions, international Arctic research institutes.

Responsible Organizations: Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S), supported by WMO and services such as DMI.

D1.7: New Near-Real-Time Data and Time Series from Drifting Observatories

Legacy Value: Provides continuous, real-time observations critical for monitoring rapid Arctic environmental changes.

Legacy Recommendations: Immediate: Secure funding and logistical support. Mid-Term: Strengthen partnerships for continuity. Long-Term: Institutionalize observations within global networks.

Concrete Next Step: Establish formal partnerships and agreements for sustained deployment and data integration.

Stakeholders: WMO, Copernicus, SAON, national weather services, maritime organizations, Arctic research institutions, community-based programs.

Responsible Organizations: Norwegian Polar Institute (NPI) in cooperation with ESA, SAON, and maritime organizations.

D1.8: Roadmap for Future Long-Term Arctic Observing System

Legacy Value: Outlines the strategic evolution and sustainability measures required for a comprehensive pan-AOSS, guiding future observation planning.

Legacy Recommendations: Immediate: Disseminate the roadmap. Mid-Term: Integrate its elements into national and international policies. Long-Term: Formalize the roadmap with sustained funding and collaboration.

Concrete Next Step: Officially present and promote the roadmap at international forums such as the Arctic Science Ministerial.

Stakeholders: SAON, EU Polar Cluster, Arctic Council working groups, national observation networks, Indigenous organizations.

Responsible Organizations: SAON in partnership with EU Polar Cluster and national research institutions, possibly facilitated by the University of Lund.

Work Package 2

D2.1: Data Management Plan (living document revised annually) (T2.1)

Legacy Value: Ensures consistent, structured, and interoperable data management across Arctic data systems for long-term sustainability.

Legacy Recommendations: Engage stakeholders (ADC, SAON, FMI, national data centers, EU initiatives) and integrate DMP practices within broader frameworks.

Concrete Next Step: Organize a dedicated workshop with ADC and SAON.

Responsible Organizations: FMI in collaboration with SAON and the Arctic Data Committee (ADC).

D2.2: Report on Interoperability Status of Copernicus Services (T2.4)

Legacy Value: Provides insights and recommendations to enhance data interoperability within Copernicus services and Polar Data architectures.

Legacy Recommendations: Regular updates and engagement with EU institutions are essential.

Concrete Next Step: Present the recommendations at an EU Polar Cluster meeting.

Responsible Organizations: ECMWF and ESA in collaboration with METNO.

D2.3: Website with Data Information, Web Services, and FAIRified Dataset Updates (T2.5)

Legacy Value: Centralizes access to Arctic data services while promoting FAIR data principles to enhance reuse.

Legacy Recommendations: Maintain active collaboration with data providers and institutionalize regular updates.

Concrete Next Step: Establish a long-term hosting and maintenance agreement with an established Arctic data organization.

Stakeholders: METNO, SAON, ADC.

Responsible Organizations: METNO, with potential transfer to SAON or ADC.

D2.4: Synthesis Reports on the Status of Arctic Data System (T2.2)

Legacy Value: Provides essential updates and evaluations on Arctic data systems, highlighting capabilities and gaps.

Legacy Recommendations: Ensure periodic continuation and integrate findings into international strategies.

Concrete Next Step: Organize a structured handover meeting with SAON and ADC.

Responsible Organizations: AMAP in cooperation with ADC and SAON.

D2.5: Updated Mapping of the Arctic Data System (T2.1)

Legacy Value: Offers a comprehensive overview of available Arctic data, enhancing transparency and efficiency.

Legacy Recommendations: Regular updates and strategic planning based on the mapping are recommended.

Concrete Next Step: Transfer mapping responsibilities to SAON and ADC for ongoing updates.

Responsible Organizations: AMAP supported by ADC and SAON.

D2.6: MeteoIO Docker Image and OGC WPS Service (T2.3)

Legacy Value: Ensures uniform and interoperable processing of Arctic meteorological, oceanographic, and glaciological data.

Legacy Recommendations: Promote widespread adoption and institutionalize the toolset within existing data processing infrastructures.

Concrete Next Step: Provide training and workshops for data managers.

Responsible Organizations: WSL/SLF and METNO.

D2.7: Final Report on Number of Long-term Preserved Datasets (T2.6)

Legacy Value: Ensures the long-term availability and usability of critical Arctic datasets, safeguarding historical continuity.

Legacy Recommendations: Develop structured preservation strategies and integrate them within global frameworks.

Concrete Next Step: Establish a formal long-term preservation agreement with recognized data repositories.

Responsible Organizations: AWI (PANGAEA) and FMI, working with EOSC and SAON.

Work Package 3

D3.2: Roadmap for optimal sensor network for monitoring EAV/SAVs for land ice (T3.4)

Legacy Value: Provides a strategic framework for combining satellite and in-situ sensor networks to monitor Arctic land ice effectively.

Legacy Recommendations: Involve key stakeholders (ESA, NASA, SAON, EPB, national research institutes) and integrate recommendations into existing observation frameworks.

Concrete Next Step: Convene an international expert workshop to review, validate, and formalize the roadmap.

Responsible Organizations: University of Bristol (UNIVBRIS) initially, then SAON and ESA.

Work Package 4

D4.1: Lake Ice Service Readiness Report (T4.8, SYKE)

Legacy Value: Offers critical insights into the feasibility and readiness of an Arctic lake ice monitoring service.

Legacy Recommendations: Integrate the service into operational monitoring systems and involve stakeholders (SYKE, national ice services, local authorities).

Concrete Next Step: SYKE organizes stakeholder consultations to integrate the service into national forecasting systems.

Responsible Organizations: SYKE in collaboration with national meteorological institutes (FMI, METNO).

D4.2: Disturbance Map for Pan-Arctic (Permafrost thawing) (T4.2, AWI)

Legacy Value: Provides a critical visual tool to illustrate permafrost thawing trends, essential for research and infrastructure safety.

Legacy Recommendations: Integrate maps into international databases and ensure continuous updates via stakeholder engagement.

Concrete Next Step: Secure long-term hosting arrangements through partnerships with SAON and ESA CCI+ Permafrost.

Responsible Organizations: AWI initially, transitioning to SAON and ESA CCI+.

D4.3: Documentation on INFRA service functionalities (T4.4, CNR)

Legacy Value: Clearly describes the functionalities for the INFRA wildfire risk management system, enabling future replication and operational use.

Legacy Recommendations: Engage relevant Arctic Council Working Groups (CAFF, EPPR) and integrate the functionalities into emergency management.

Concrete Next Step: Conduct stakeholder workshops to adopt and integrate INFRA functionalities.

Responsible Organizations: CNR, supported by CAFF, EPPR, and Copernicus EFFIS.

D4.4: Regional Sea Ice Information Tool (T4.3, METNO/NPI)

Legacy Value: Provides detailed, region-specific sea ice information and forecasts critical for shipping, fisheries, and coastal safety.

Legacy Recommendations: Institutionalize the tool within operational sea ice forecasting systems and align data streams with Copernicus Marine Service.

Concrete Next Step: Initiate a formal agreement between METNO/NPI and Copernicus Marine Service for sustained hosting.

Responsible Organizations: METNO and NPI.

D4.5: Development of Community Event Databases and Stakeholder Workshops (T4.1, Snowchange)

Legacy Value: Compiles indigenous and local knowledge with scientific data, bridging both for holistic Arctic understanding.

Legacy Recommendations: Maintain integration with SDWG and secure long-term funding to support continuous database updates.

Concrete Next Step: Host a legacy planning workshop involving SDWG, Arctic Indigenous communities, and research organizations.

Responsible Organizations: Snowchange, transitioning to SDWG and local Indigenous groups.

D4.6: New Local Baseline Data on Marine Climate and Noisescape (T4.7, GINR)

Legacy Value: Establishes crucial baseline information on marine ecosystems and noisescape impacts for future ecological management.

Legacy Recommendations: Embed observations into Greenlandic national monitoring programs and regional ecosystem assessments.

Concrete Next Step: Host a stakeholder workshop with GINR and local communities to commit to ongoing observations.
